Autism Ambassador meets a known expert for Autism

One of our Autism Ambassadors, Sasha Willden is a student in BSc Creative Computing at Goldsmiths, University of London. She met with a well-known expert, Francesca Happé at King's College London who specialises in the field of Autism Spectrum Disorders.

Sasha met with Francesca to discuss about with Autism, what to do after University and what support there is currently for Autistic people. Importantly, the meeting for Sasha was to reflect about Autism, her University progress as well as what to do after her degree, types of services and career. Sasha is wanting to develop in the near future applications, web plugins and other technical services to support those with the Autism spectrum disorder and other associated disorders.

Sasha is currently working on a Javascript plugin tab in web development and in her own time for web-based strategies for users have less burdens on websites to help those who are on the spectrum, associated disorders and other disabilities such as: visually impairment needing to increase/decrease font sizes, dyslexia to help with seeing clearly by change the colour of the background, colour blinded people who need to see the background or font in a different colour etc.

The Javascript plugin tab is currently live, will continue to be improved and can be viewed at Autism Ambassadors.

Sasha will continue to developmental projects similar to this one in her degree, hoping to network with experts for advice in that for her projects and to, later on, do an MSc Cognitive Neuroscience and after an PhD in an similar discipline (undecided what or where).

Blue Badge

There was good news last week for drivers among the ASD community as the government extended the blue badge disabled parking scheme to include ‘hidden disabilities’.

The Department of Transport has confirmed that from 2019, people with hidden disabilities - including autism, will be able to apply for blue badge parking permits which will enable them to park closer to their destinations. The scheme began in 1970 and around 2.4 million people in England have a permit.
